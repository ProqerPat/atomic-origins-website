Discovering Nuclear Fission

The discovery of nuclear fission marked a turning point in modern physics, showing that splitting an atom could release vast amounts of energy. Many early 20th-century scientists worked to combine theoretical pysics with experiments to unlock the atom's secrets. Their work laid the foundation for both scientific breakthroughs and the development of the atomic bomb, forever changing the way humanity understood energy and matter.

Here are some of the key scientists whose research made nuclear fission possible.
  • Albert Einstein
  • Enrico Fermi
  • Lise Meitner
  • Otto Hahn

Albert Einstein provided the theoretical groundwork for nuclear energy with his famous mass-energy equivalence, E=mc², which helped scientists understand the potential energy stored in atoms and guided early nuclear research.

Enrico Fermi conducted neutron bombardment experiments that revealed how atomic nuclei could be split, laying the experimental foundation for nuclear fission and influencing the future of atomic research.

Lise Meitner interpreted the results of nuclear experiments and explained the process of nuclear fission, helping the scientific community understand how uranium nuclei could be split to insane release energy.

Otto Hahn conducted the actual splitting of the uranium nucleus through his experiments, providing the concrete evidence of nuclear fission that confirmed theoretical predictions and advanced atomic research, eventually leading to the Manhattan Project.
